About the Event

About StraveX'26

StraveX'26 is a solution-driven intra-college technical hackathon organized by the Department of Artificial Intelligence & Data Science and the Entrepreneurship Development Cell. It is designed to identify overlooked system-level challenges and transform them into intelligent, scalable solutions.

Open to all students across departments and all years, this presentation-based hackathon encourages interdisciplinary collaboration, structured innovation, and real-world problem solving. Refreshments, certificates, and prizes await all participants.

How It Works

Hackathon Format

A structured, presentation-focused event that rewards research, innovation, and technical thinking.

Solution Presentation-Based

Teams research a real-world problem, develop a structured solution, and present in front of expert judges. Clear communication and analytical thinking are as important as the solution.

PPT Submission Mandatory

All teams must submit a structured PowerPoint presentation using the official StraveX'26 template by 22 March via event email. The PPT forms the basis of evaluation.

Prototype Optional

Working prototypes are not mandatory but earn extra evaluation weightage. Teams that build and demonstrate solutions will receive bonus credit.

Guidelines

Rules & Eligibility

  • Open to all students from any department and any year of study.
  • Maximum of 5 members per team. Solo participation is allowed.
  • Interdisciplinary teams are strongly encouraged.
  • All team members must be currently enrolled students of the institution.
  • Each student may register with only one team. Duplicate registrations will be disqualified.
  • Registration fee of ₹200 per team is mandatory for confirmation.
  • A PPT submission is mandatory — teams without it will not be evaluated.
  • Use the official StraveX'26 template. Deviating from the structure will result in score deduction.
  • PPT must be emailed to the event address by 22 March 2026, 11:59 PM.
  • Prototype or demo is optional but earns extra evaluation weightage.
  • Late submissions will not be accepted under any circumstances.

Presentation Quality

All participants must maintain professional and respectful conduct throughout the event. Plagiarism, dishonesty, or misrepresentation will lead to immediate disqualification.

Teams must present original ideas. Recycled or previously submitted projects are ineligible. AI tools may assist research but cannot generate the full solution.

Judges' and organizing committee decisions are final. Disputes must be raised with the faculty coordinator before results are announced.

Evaluation

Judging Criteria

Each submission is evaluated across five equally-weighted dimensions by a panel of expert judges. Aim for excellence in every area.

  • Innovation
  • Technical Feasibility
  • Impact
  • Scalability
  • Presentation Quality
